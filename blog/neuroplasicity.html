Neuroplasticity

Research Neuroplasticity * Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people by showing them that your brain is able to be remoulded. We are able to unlearn behaviour patterns that don’t benefit us in our current environment and learn new ones that do. Understanding neuroplasicity gives people an edge when learning new skills compared to those that don’t purely because they understand that they are not ever fully stuck in their self imposed ways.

* Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

I think I have been using neuroplasicity for a while. Last year I unlearned bad eating habits and learned better ones. I have unlearned heavy substance abuse and made the gym a habit and have learned a lot about my body in the process. I just didn’t have a name for it. Now that I do know about it. I can use it by figuring out and unlearning old habits and consciously trying to replace those with new skills and more beneficial habits.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Practise, practise, practise. Constant repetition of activities helps build stronger, thicker and more hard wired connections in the brain.

Sleeping well, eating healthy, drinking water and exercising puts your body and mind into the best state possible to be able to learn.

Understanding neuroplasicity as much as possible by learning how the brain works actually puts you in better to increase it. Understanding the information in front of you. Its one thing to recite word for word what is said but if you can put the information into your own words and actively practise the information then you’ll be much better off

Travelling: seeing other peoples perspectives gives you deep insight into how the world works. If you stay at home you will be stuck in an echo chamber and you may never learn anything new.

Creating: gives you the opportunity to make mistake and learn from them.

Learning an instrument rewires your brain a lot. From hand eye coordination to timing awareness to even understanding music on a deeper level mechanical level understanding why certain songs trigger certain emotion. Very helpful.
